# Information Security Strategy for SMEs (Risk, Recovery & Awareness)

> **Author:** Rohit Malik | **Domain:** Cybersecurity | **Type:** Academic Project | **Purpose:** Portfolio & Educational

---

## Overview

This project explores the importance of information security management in small to medium-sized enterprises (SMEs), with a focus on practical and cost-effective ways to improve cybersecurity posture.

The work examines how SMEs can strengthen resilience through risk awareness, incident response planning, disaster recovery preparation, and employee training, even when operating with limited technical and financial resources.

---

## Project Objective

The objective of this project is to analyse the need for structured information security management in SMEs and propose practical strategies to improve resilience against cyber threats.

The project focuses on three core areas:

- Information security justification in SMEs
- Incident response and disaster recovery planning
- Security training and awareness

---

## Key Areas Covered

### 1. Information Security in SMEs

- SMEs are increasingly exposed to cyber threats due to weaker controls and limited security resources
- The absence of dedicated IT or security teams increases organisational risk
- Information security affects not only technical systems, but also business continuity, operations, and reputation
- Frameworks such as NIST and ISO can be adapted to suit SME environments

### 2. Incident Response and Disaster Recovery

- Structured response plans are essential for handling cyber incidents effectively
- Incident Response (IR) and Disaster Recovery (DR) serve different but connected purposes
- Backup strategies and recovery planning are critical for reducing downtime and data loss
- Business Impact Analysis (BIA) helps prioritise systems and recovery efforts

### 3. Security Training and Awareness

- Human error remains a major cause of security incidents
- Security Education, Training, and Awareness (SETA) improves employee readiness
- Phishing and social engineering are common attack methods targeting SMEs
- Continuous awareness programs help strengthen security culture across the organisation
